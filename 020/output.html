# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training Athlete

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete training for a marathon with dairy-free dietary requirements:

* **Calorie Target:** 3,500 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (525g), Fat (525g), Carbs (2,100g)

**Meal Plan:**

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), almond butter (25g), and coconut milk (240ml).
  * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Energy bar (50g) with dates (80g) and almonds (30g).
  * Protein: 12g, Fat: 14g, Carbs: 65g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Grilled chicken breast (180g), brown rice (150g cooked), steamed broccoli (200g), and olive oil dressing (15ml).
  * Protein: 48g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 75g

* **Pre-Training Snack (3:30 PM):** White bread (80g), honey (30g), and peanut butter (20g).
  * Protein: 10g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 60g

* **Post-Training Snack (5:30 PM):** Sweet potato (200g) with lean ground turkey (120g) and vegetables (100g mixed).
  * Protein: 32g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 55g

* **Dinner (7:30 PM):** Baked salmon (160g), quinoa (120g cooked), roasted asparagus (150g), and olive oil (10ml).
  * Protein: 38g, Fat: 16g, Carbs: 65g

* **Evening Snack (9:30 PM):** Rice cakes (60g), almond butter (20g), and banana (100g).
  * Protein: 12g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 65g

This meal plan emphasizes complex carbohydrates for sustained energy, lean proteins for muscle recovery, and healthy fats for hormone production—all essential for marathon training success while avoiding all dairy products.
