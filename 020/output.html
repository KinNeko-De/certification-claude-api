## Daily Nutrition Summary

- **Carbohydrates:** 650-700g (85% of diet) — prioritized for fueling
- **Protein:** 110-120g (15% of diet)
- **Fat:** 60-70g (20% of diet)
- **Hydration:** 3-4L total (before, during, after run)

---

## MEAL PLAN

### **BREAKFAST (6:00-6:30 AM)** — Pre-Run Carb Load
*2-3 hours before run | ~700 kcal, 130g carbs*

- **2 cups oatmeal** (cooked with water or low-fat milk)
- **2 tbsp honey**
- **1 sliced banana**
- **200 ml low-fat yogurt**
- **Pinch of cinnamon**
- **200 ml water or herbal tea**

*Why: Easily digestible oats + simple carbs from honey/banana provide sustained energy; low fiber/fat for easy digestion.*

---

### **PRE-RUN FUEL (30 min before, ~8:00 AM)** — Quick Carbs
*~200 kcal, 50g carbs | Consumed 30 minutes before start*

- **1 energy gel or 1 sports bar** (100-120 kcal)
- **1 medium banana or 200 ml sports drink**
- **250 ml water**

*Why: Simple sugars digest quickly and won't cause GI distress during the run.*

---

### **DURING RUN (8:30-10:30 AM)** — Sustain Energy
*For a 20+ km run (~2 hours), fuel every 45 minutes*

Every 45 minutes during the run:
- **1 energy gel (100 kcal, 25g carbs)** OR
- **Sports drink (6-8% carb solution, 150-200 ml, ~30-40g carbs)**
- **Water (200-300 ml between fuel)**

*Total during run: ~200 kcal, 80-100g carbs*

*Why: 30-60g carbs/hour is standard for endurance; this prevents bonking and maintains pace.*

---

### **POST-RUN RECOVERY (10:45 AM, within 30 min of finish)** — Refuel & Repair
*~500 kcal, 80g carbs, 25g protein*

- **Chocolate milk (500 ml, 2% fat)** — convenient 3:1 carb-to-protein ratio
- **OR smoothie:**
  - 250 ml low-fat milk
  - 1 scoop whey protein powder
  - 1.5 cups blueberries
  - 1 banana
  - 1 tbsp honey

*Why: Carbs + protein within 30 min maximizes glycogen resynthesis and muscle repair.*

---

### **BRUNCH (12:00 PM)** — Main Recovery Meal
*~800 kcal, 120g carbs, 30g protein*

- **2 slices whole-grain toast**
- **2 eggs (scrambled or fried)**
- **1 cup diced pineapple or melon**
- **1 tbsp almond butter**
- **250 ml orange juice**
- **Small side salad with olive oil dressing**

*Why: Whole grains + protein + fresh fruit continues glycogen restoration.*

---

### **AFTERNOON SNACK (3:00 PM)** — Energy Top-Up
*~250 kcal, 45g carbs, 10g protein*

- **1 large apple with 1.5 tbsp peanut butter**
- **100g Greek yogurt**
- **Handful of granola**

*Why: Balanced snack maintains energy levels in afternoon recovery phase.*

---

### **DINNER (6:30 PM)** — Evening Recovery & Balance
*~900 kcal, 120g carbs, 35g protein*

- **200g grilled salmon or lean chicken breast**
- **1.5 cups white or jasmine rice** (easier to digest than brown rice on heavy training days)
- **Roasted vegetables (broccoli, carrots, sweet potato)**
- **Side: Olive oil + lemon dressing**
- **Small bowl mixed berries**

*Why: High-quality protein for muscle repair; white rice is easily digestible; omega-3s (if salmon) aid recovery.*

---

### **EVENING SNACK (8:30 PM, optional)** — Light Recovery
*~200 kcal, 35g carbs, 15g protein*

- **1 cup low-fat milk**
- **2 oatmeal cookies**
- **1 small banana**

*Why: Light carbs + protein before sleep supports overnight glycogen synthesis.*
